🇩🇪GermanyLive

Regulation §1e StVG (2021) — Europe's most advanced national AV law. Allows fully driverless L4 operation in defined areas without a safety driver, subject to KBA approval. Already used by MOIA.
Strategic note▾
Germany's §1e StVG is the gold standard for European AV regulation — the only national law that permits commercial L4 driverless operation on public roads. This has made Germany the primary target for every major global AV player entering Europe. Hamburg and Berlin are the two most active AV cities in continental Europe. Momenta/Uber and Apollo Go/Lyft launching in Munich in 2026 will make Germany the most competitive AV market in the EU.

🇬🇧United KingdomLive / Scaling
Wayve × Uber — London robotaxi (Spring 2026) ↗Waymo — London commercial (2026) ↗Apollo Go / Lyft — UK (2026) ↗Fusion Processing — L4 programme
Regulation Automated Vehicles Act 2024 — the most comprehensive AV legislation in Europe. UK Secretary of State's accelerated self-driving pilot framework (June 2025) fast-tracked Wayve/Uber and Waymo permitting. DVSA oversight.
Strategic note▾
The UK is the most competitive AV market in Europe in 2026 — three separate robotaxi deployments launching simultaneously (Wayve, Waymo, Apollo Go). The Automated Vehicles Act 2024 is the most complete AV legal framework in Europe, explicitly defining liability and creating a commercial pathway. Post-Brexit, the UK can move faster than EU member states on AV regulation. London is set to become Europe's first city with multiple competing commercial robotaxi services.
